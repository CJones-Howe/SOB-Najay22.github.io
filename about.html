Hatha Yoga

Hatha is a general term for physical yoga that includes postures and breathwork.
-A Hatha class typically includes a mix of standing and seated poses.
-The focus is on strengthening, relaxation, and flexibility.
-Poses are held for several breaths.
-It’s great for beginners, teaching the foundations of yoga and breath/body awareness.

Yin Yoga

-Slow, meditative, floor-based yoga
-Caters to stillness and rest.
-long, deep, active stretching
-It targets the flexibility of connective tissues as well
-Use of props to help you get into position.

Iyengar Yoga

Bellur Krishnamachar Sundararaja Iyengar, the founder of Iyengar Yoga. He was one of the most influential yoga teachers of the 20th century and played a major role in introducing yoga to the West.
-Emphasizes precision and alignment
-A slow, strong practice with standing and seated poses held for longer periods
-Use of props (chairs, blocks, straps) to support alignment
-Suitable for all ages and skill levels, including those with injuries or physical limitations

Vinyasa yoga

-AKA Flow yoga
-Quick paced
-Repetitive poses
-Breathe focused movement
-For more experienced participants

Bikram Yoga

-AKA Hot Yoga
-Takes place in heated locations
-More intense and challenging
-Heat allows more flexibility

Ashtanga Yoga

-Ashtanga yoga is a dynamic, physically demanding style with six progressive levels
-Each level has a fixed, structured sequence of poses
-Emphasizes breath control and focused movement through postures
-Best suited for those who enjoy a rigorous, disciplined practice

Power Yoga

-Power yoga is similar to Ashtanga yoga but without a fixed sequence of poses
-It’s a flowing, athletic practice that provides a full-body workout
-Emphasizes linking breath with movement
-Often practiced in heated rooms to boost sweating
-Ideal for burning calories and building strength and endurance
